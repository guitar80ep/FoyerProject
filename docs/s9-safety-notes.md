# §9 · SAFETY NOTES

_Part of the 2022 Morgan Lane Foyer Remodel Project — [guitar80ep/FoyerProject](https://github.com/guitar80ep/FoyerProject)_

---

_General safety by hazard type. Phase-specific safety warnings stay in §4. Two to three sentences per note._

_Sort: alphabetical by hazard type heading._

## Chemical (Epoxy Products)

Two-part epoxy grout and epoxy mortar require gloves and eye protection. Hardener component is a sensitizer — repeated skin contact causes allergic reactions. Mix in ventilated area.

## Chemical (Membrane Primer & Mastic)

Polyglass Polytack SA primer and PG 500 mastic contain solvents. Use in well-ventilated conditions — the foyer's semi-outdoor configuration provides natural ventilation, but avoid prolonged inhalation in still air. Wear nitrile gloves and safety glasses during application.

## Electrical

Use non-contact voltage tester and circuit tracer to identify and label every wire before demo. Do not assume a wire is dead because a breaker appears to be off. Turn off breaker and test before any work. GFCI required for semi-outdoor outlet.

## Fall Protection (Parapet Work)

Setting coping at the parapet edge requires leaning over the outer face. Use a stable work platform (scaffold plank or outrigger platform) rather than leaning from a ladder. Have a spotter when working at the edge.

## Metal Handling (Aluminum Flashing)

Freshly cut Kynar-coated aluminum scuppers and flashing have sharp edges. Use cut-resistant gloves when handling and fabricating aluminum components. Kynar coating can be scratched during cutting and bending — use a fine-tooth blade and protect finished surfaces from tool marks.

## PPE (General)

N95 or P100 respirator for demolition, sanding, and tile cutting. Safety glasses at all times during power tool use. Knee pads for flooring/tile work. Hearing protection with power tools.

## Self-Adhered Membrane (Handling)

Self-adhered SBS membrane bonds on contact — once the release film is removed and the sheet is pressed to the substrate, repositioning is extremely difficult. Dry-fit and align each sheet before peeling the release film. Work with a partner on larger sheets to avoid wrinkles and misalignment. Roll all laps and seams firmly with a J-roller.

## Silica Dust (Tile Cutting)

Porcelain contains crystalline silica. Cutting produces fine particles causing silicosis with cumulative exposure. Use wet saw at all times. Wear NIOSH-approved N95 or P100 respirator even with wet saw. NEVER dry-cut porcelain, ceramic, or stone tile.

## Structural Instability (Demo)

Foyer has confirmed structural movement. During demo, walls and roof components may shift unexpectedly when load paths are disturbed. Install temporary shoring under roof deck before removing any wall cladding or sheathing. Do not work under potentially unstable roof structure without shoring. Never work alone during demo phase.
