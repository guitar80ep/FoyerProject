## §2.1 · Foyer Status

The foyer entryway is **CLOSED TO OCCUPANTS**. The garage entry is being used instead. Two simultaneous signs of active structural failure are present: horizontal tile cracks across wall faces (indicating vertical settlement or wall base drop) and a front door that binds and will not open/close normally (indicating frame racking from structural deflection). Maintain this closure restriction until the SE assessment is complete and all structural repairs are approved by the City inspector.

> ⛔ **STOP:** PRIORITY 0 — STRUCTURAL ASSESSMENT REQUIRED BEFORE ANY WORK BEGINS. The SE must physically inspect the foyer in its current deformed state BEFORE demo begins. Once demolition starts, the failure pattern is destroyed and the SE cannot determine the root cause or the full scope of structural repair needed. Do not pull permits or begin any work until an SE has issued a written assessment.

> 📋 **NOTE:** Before the SE visit, check whether the homeowner's insurance policy covers structural damage from water infiltration or progressive deterioration. Some policies have specific exclusions or time limits. If coverage is possible, document the existing damage thoroughly with photographs and video before any remediation begins.
