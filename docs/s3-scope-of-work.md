### §3.A · Low-Porosity Porcelain Tile Cladding

| Attribute     | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| Description   | Replace failed travertine with rectified porcelain tile on all wall surfaces and parapet faces |
| Key Specs     | Tile: rectified porcelain, full-body, ≤0.5% absorption (ANSI A137.1 Group V, Impervious), PEI IV or V, matte/honed finish, frost-resistant (ANSI A137.1 F-rated). Format: 24″×24″ or 24″×48″ walls; 12″×24″ planters. Style: travertine-look, warm buff/ivory/gray. Thinset: ANSI A118.4 polymer-modified, 95% min. back-butter coverage. Grout: epoxy, non-porous, salt-resistant; sanded ≥1/8″ joints, unsanded <1/8″. Movement joints: TCNA EJ171, color-matched marine silicone at inside corners, wall-to-floor transitions, max 12 ft o.c. |
| Standards     | ANSI A108, ANSI A118.4, ANSI A137.1, ASTM C920 Class 50 (silicone), TCNA EJ171 → see §10 |
| Status        | [TBD] — Specific tile style/color pending selection and Planning Dept. Condition 23 confirmation |

> **Rationale:** Porcelain at ≤0.5% absorption eliminates the fundamental porosity failure of travertine. Visually identical to natural stone in modern large-format tiles. Sealing not required on tile face (grout joints still need sealer). Available in travertine-look finishes that satisfy Planning Condition 23 warm stone aesthetics. Alternatives rejected: sealed natural stone (sealing degrades in coastal UV/salt); ceramic tile (lower durability, higher absorption); stucco (different aesthetic, not consistent with original approval).

> **Rationale (epoxy grout):** Epoxy grout is non-porous and impervious to salt water. Standard Portland-based grout absorbs moisture and salt ions, leading to spalling and cracking within a few years at coastal locations. Working time is approximately 20–30 minutes at typical SoCal temperatures — mix only what can be placed and cleaned in that window. Remove from tile face before it skins over. Alternatives rejected: unsanded cementitious (absorptive, salt-vulnerable); polymer-modified cementitious (acceptable but still absorptive vs. epoxy).

### §3.B · Full Tear-Down, Framing Repair & Wall Assembly Rebuild

| Attribute     | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| Description   | Demo to structural frame. SE assesses first. Replace rotted sheathing, damaged sill plates, wet framing. Rebuild wall assembly: sheathing → WRB → drainage mat → backer board → thinset → tile. 316 SS weep screed at all wall bases. |
| Key Specs     | Sill plates: PT ACQ or CA-B rated lumber. All framing within 6″ of grade/concrete must be PT. Replace any member with visible rot, soft spots, or >25% section loss — full length, no splice repair. Borate treatment (Tim-bor or equiv.) on all retained framing before sheathing. Sheathing: 5/8″ CDX plywood, nailed per CBC shear wall schedule. WRB: Henry Blueskin VP100 or equiv., shingle-lapped min. 6″. Drainage mat: Benjamin Obdyke Slicker Classic, 3/8″ gap. Backer board: HardieBacker ½″ (ASTM C1325), 316 SS screws per manufacturer schedule. Weep screed: 316 SS V-notch, min. 3/4″ ground face, min. 2″ above finished paving, 316 SS fasteners at 6″ o.c. max, 6″ min. laps sealed with flashing tape. |
| Standards     | AWC NDS 2018 (member sizing), CBC 2022 Chapter 16 (SDC D), CBC 2022 Chapter 23 (shear wall schedule), ASTM C1325 (backer board) → see §10 |
| Status        | [PENDING-SE] — Full framing scope cannot be defined until SE report is in hand |

> **Rationale:** The original travertine was set directly against the substrate with no WRB, no drainage mat, and no weep screed. Any water that penetrated the stone had nowhere to go and accumulated against the wood framing. The new wall assembly creates a complete drainage plane: the WRB blocks bulk water, the drainage mat creates an air gap for water to channel downward, and the weep screed provides a defined exit point at the wall base. This is the critical assembly correction that prevents the original failure mode from recurring.

> **Rationale (316 SS requirement):** All exterior metal fasteners, screws, clips, and weep screeds in this project must be 316 stainless steel. 316 SS contains molybdenum which provides superior resistance to chloride (salt) corrosion. 304 SS — the more common grade — pits and corrodes in coastal salt air within 2–5 years. "SS" or "stainless" without a grade specification almost always means 304. Always verify the grade before accepting any stainless steel product for this project.

### §3.C · Aluminum Kynar Standing Seam Roof, Gutter & Rain Chain

| Attribute     | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| Description   | Flat deck re-framed to 5″ rise (½″:12″), draining toward street. McClelroy Maxima 3″ structural standing seam panels with 4″ K-style aluminum Kynar gutter and aluminum cube rain chain. |
| Key Specs     | **Panels:** Kynar 500 / PVDF 70% resin min. coating, Antique White / Classic Ivory, striated finish (preferred — reduces oil canning visibility). 11–12 panels, 18″ width, 126″ length. Single 90° mechanical seam — hydrostatic system rated to ¼″:12″ minimum. Maxima 3 return-leg seam geometry (highest uplift performer in Maxima family, 200+ mph rated). **Clips:** Fixed low clips, 1′ o.c. uniform across entire roof (~110 clips). Confirm clip part number and FM/UL uplift compliance with McElroy rep in writing. ZAC fastener coastal suitability to be confirmed — aluminum upgrade at eave/closure locations if required for <1 mile from ocean. **Accessories:** Eave plate (McElroy-supplied, per McElroy Detail C-2). Outside closures (~12, confirm installation direction per McElroy Detail C-2). Double bead tape sealant 3/16″ × 7/8″ continuous + pigtails per McElroy Detail C-2. **Substrate:** 5/8″ CDX plywood deck + ASTM D1970 self-adhering underlayment + synthetic slip sheet + panels. Rigid insulation only if Title 24 requires. **Seamer:** McElroy electric seamer rented from Developmental Industries (mcelroyseamers.com) — no substitutions, voids all warranties. **Slope:** ½″:12″ (5″ rise over 122″ run). Building/interior side HIGH, street side LOW. Actual slant length = 122.1″; panel length stays at 126″. **Gutter:** 4″ K-style aluminum, Kynar 500 / PVDF coating, Matte Black. Profile matches existing house gutters. Run: ~230″ along street-facing low edge. Hidden aluminum hangers with neoprene washer face at 24″ o.c. max (~12 hangers). Sizing: 97 sq ft × 2″/hr design rain = ~0.023 cu ft/sec peak — 4″ K-style handles with margin. All gutter fasteners: aluminum screws with neoprene bonded washers. Confirm Kynar 500 (not polyester) coating in writing from supplier before ordering — verify with physical Matte Black sample in outdoor light alongside tile and panel samples. **Overflow:** Aluminum Kynar scupper on high/building side, Matte Black, set 2″ above gutter inlet elevation. **Rain chain:** Monarch Abode Multi Cube aluminum cube rain chain, SKU #18107, 16-gauge aluminum, black powder-coat finish, cube cup-style (alternating 2″ and 1.5″ square cups), 8.5 ft standard length. Connects to K-style gutter outlet via included triangular gutter clip — confirm clip fits K-style outlet without adapter before ordering. Terminates at rock garden bed (min. 2 ft × 2 ft, 6–8″ deep, ½″–¾″ river rock over filter fabric). Measure drop height before ordering — order additional chain unit if drop exceeds 8.5 ft. Drainage direction TBD → see §6. All flashings: aluminum Kynar 500 per §3.H. |
| Standards     | ASTM D1970 (self-adhering underlayment) → see §10 for governmental standards. **Manufacturer references (installation only):** Kynar 500 / PVDF 70% resin min. (panel and gutter coating specification — 30+ year coastal rated); McElroy Detail C-2 (eave plate, outside closure, and sealant tape installation); FM/UL uplift compliance per McElroy clip confirmation letter. |
| Status        | [TBD] — Panel count finalized after demo field measurements. McElroy rep confirmation outstanding → see §6. Gutter Kynar 500 Matte Black supplier to be confirmed → see §6. **McElroy Rep Pre-Order Checklist (complete before ordering):** (1) 11–12 panels, 18″ width, 126″ length, Antique White/Classic Ivory Kynar 500, striated; (2) fixed low clips — confirm part #, ~110 count, FM/UL uplift compliance in writing; (3) eave plate quantity for both zones; (4) outside closures (~12) — confirm installation direction per Detail C-2; (5) double bead tape sealant quantity; (6) ZAC fastener coastal suitability — confirm in writing or upgrade to aluminum; (7) mechanical seamer rental reservation at mcelroyseamers.com; (8) weathertightness warranty — confirm certified installer requirement; (9) written confirmation ½″:12″ slope above ¼″:12″ hydrostatic minimum — for permit file; (10) confirm 18″ Maxima 3 availability and lead time at Peachtree City, GA. |

> **Rationale:** Structural standing seam is the highest-performing low-slope roofing system available. The 3″ rib height channels water efficiently even at minimal slope. Hidden clip fasteners eliminate penetrations through the panel face — zero exposed fasteners in the roof field. Kynar 500 coating is rated for 30+ years in coastal environments (standard polyester begins chalking within 5–10 years in salt air). The original flat tiled roof had no proper membrane beneath it; tile joints on a low-slope roof are not waterproof. Alternatives rejected: modified bitumen membrane (requires replacement every 15–20 years); TPO/EPDM (less elegant, visible seams); barrel tile (too heavy for existing framing, wrong aesthetic).

> **Rationale (slope direction):** Minimum code slope is ¼″:12″ (~2.5″ rise over 122″). Owner elected 5″ (½″:12″) for margin. Draining toward street puts the gutter and rain chain at the most visible, accessible location — the street-facing entry — as a design feature. Building side being higher means debris-blockage ponding stays away from the building wall. Alternatives rejected: slope toward building (gutter less accessible, pools water against building); center drain (requires roof penetration, adds leak risk).

> **Rationale (aluminum Kynar system — gutter, rain chain, and flashings):** All metal components in the drainage and flashing system use aluminum as the base metal, eliminating galvanic corrosion risk by design rather than relying on coating integrity or isolation hardware. Kynar 500 (PVDF 70% resin minimum) provides 30+ year coastal performance on the panels — the same coating on the gutter and flashings creates a single unified material system. Alternatives rejected: copper (premium cost, galvanic risk at interfaces, patina aesthetic no longer the design driver); 316 SS mixed with aluminum (galvanic couple in salt-air environment even with isolation; coating breach during field fabrication creates corrosion risk). K-style profile selected over half-round to match existing house gutters and provide a native fit for the Monarch Abode triangular rain chain clip without adapter. Matte Black color selected to contrast with Antique White / Classic Ivory panels — the black gutter and chain read as intentional design accent at the entry rather than a continuation of the roof color. Black powder-coat on the rain chain is acceptable for a decorative element at this price point — it is replaceable if finish degrades, whereas the base aluminum substrate will not corrode.

> **Rationale (4″ K-style gutter sizing):** 97 sq ft roof area at 2″/hr design rain intensity produces ~0.023 cu ft/sec peak flow. 4″ K-style handles this load with margin. Profile matches existing house gutters for visual consistency. Alternatives rejected: 3″ half-round (specialty profile, incompatible with standard rain chain clip, harder to source in Kynar 500); 5″ K-style (oversized for this run, heavier, unnecessary).

| Standards     | ASTM A276/A240 (316 SS specification) → see §10 |

> **Rationale:** Hidden 316 SS clips provide full board-by-board maintenance access — any individual board can slide out and be replaced without tools, demolition, or damage to adjacent boards. Brad nails rejected: permanent connection, Thermo Radiata's denser structure splits on pry-out, steel brads rust and streak in salt air. Face screws rejected: visible fasteners break the clean look. Glue rejected: impossible to remove for maintenance. Thermo Radiata selected for dimensional stability and natural decay resistance without chemical treatment. Alternatives rejected: redwood (increasingly expensive, environmentally restricted); Western Red Cedar (less dimensionally stable); Ipe (very heavy, hard to work, expensive, tropical sourcing concerns); painted pine (would fail within a few years in coastal exposure).

| Attribute     | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| Description   | Same porcelain tile series as walls. Sloped INWARD toward roof. Mitered at corners for monolithic appearance. |
| Key Specs     | Material: same porcelain series as wall tile, 2″ mitered pieces cut at 45° from large-format tiles. Slope: INWARD toward roof, min. ¼″:12″ — water drains to roof surface, not exterior wall. | Standards     | ANSI A108, ANSI A118.4, ASTM C920 Class 50, ASTM D1970, TCNA EJ171 → see §10 |
| Status        | [TBD] — Pending tile selection and parapet framing completion |

> **Rationale:** Coping must slope toward the roof (inward) to direct rain to the drainage system rather than down the exterior wall face. The miter cut at corners creates a seamless, monolithic appearance that reads as a solid stone cap from street level — consistent with the original architecture's character. Mitered porcelain conceals all metal flashings and waterproofing from view. This is the highest-skill tile task on the project. Alternatives rejected: metal coping (utilitarian, inconsistent with stone character); bullnose tile cap (visible horizontal joint collects dirt and moisture); precast concrete coping (heavy, less precise at corners).

### §3.H · Aluminum Kynar Flashing System

| Attribute     | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| Description   | Complete flashing system for parapet base, counter-flashing, and coping. All-aluminum Kynar 500 construction — no dissimilar metals. |
| Key Specs     | **Materials:** Base flashing and counter-flashing: 0.032″ aluminum sheet, Kynar 500 / PVDF 70% resin min. coating, Antique White / Classic Ivory (color-matched to panels). Coping flashing: 0.024″ aluminum sheet, Kynar 500, Antique White / Classic Ivory. All aluminum alloy 3003-H14 or 3105-H14 — standard brake-forming grades. **Cut edge treatment:** Every field-cut edge must be treated with compatible Kynar touch-up paint or aluminum edge sealant before installation. Bare aluminum exposed at cut edges will oxidize in coastal salt air — this step is mandatory, not optional. **Fastener schedule:** Aluminum blind rivets (aluminum mandrel, 3/16″ diameter, ≥3/8″ washer face) at all lap joints, 6″ o.c. Aluminum screws (6061 alloy or equiv., #10 × 1-1/2″ min.) with neoprene bonded washers at all substrate attachments, spacing per SE wind uplift confirmation — preliminary 6″ o.c. at base flashing, 8″ o.c. at coping flashing. No 316 SS or any other non-aluminum fastener anywhere in the flashing system. **Lap joints:** Side laps min. 4″. End laps min. 6″. Continuous bead of ASTM C920 Class 25 polyurethane sealant in every lap before fastening — sealant is the primary water seal, mechanical fastener is the clamp. **Sealant spec:** ASTM C920 Class 25 polyurethane (e.g., Tremco Dymonic or equiv.) at all laps, penetrations, and transitions. ASTM C920 Class 50 marine silicone (color-matched) at all exposed terminations and movement joints. Do not mix polyurethane and silicone in the same joint. **Corner treatment:** Inside corners: pre-formed aluminum Kynar inside corner units — do not field-form inside corners, they are the highest-leak-risk location on the parapet. Outside corners: field-formed with tight miter, sealed hem, and continuous sealant bead at joint. **Integration sequence with ASTM D1970 and WRB:** (1) Install ASTM D1970 self-adhering underlayment over full roof deck; (2) Install base flashing at roof-to-parapet transition — turn up min. 8″ on parapet interior face, embed bottom edge in ASTM D1970 with min. 4″ overlap; (3) Install counter-flashing lapping over base flashing min. 4″; (4) Install WRB on parapet exterior face, lapping over counter-flashing top by min. 4″; (5) Coping flashing installed last — under mortar bed, turned up min. 1″ on both parapet faces, integrated with WRB. **Penetration sealing:** Any conduit, fastener, or pipe penetrating the flashing plane must be sealed with ASTM C920 polyurethane applied to back of penetration before driving fastener, and finished with surface sealant bead around penetration perimeter on face side. **SE confirmation required:** SE to confirm base flashing fastener pull-out spec meets wind uplift requirements for coastal Exposure Category at this parapet geometry before finalizing fastener schedule. Preliminary 6″ o.c. aluminum screw schedule may need tightening to 4″ o.c. — do not build until SE confirms. |
| Standards     | SMACNA Architectural Sheet Metal Manual 7th Ed. (flashing geometry, lap lengths, corner treatment, dissimilar metal guidance), ASTM C920 Class 25 (polyurethane sealant), ASTM C920 Class 50 (marine silicone), ASTM D1970 (underlayment integration) → see §10 |
| Status        | [PENDING-SE] — Fastener pull-out spec and final fastener schedule require SE confirmation before fabrication |

> **Rationale:** All-aluminum construction eliminates galvanic corrosion risk by design — no dissimilar metal contact anywhere in the flashing system, removing dependency on coating integrity or isolation hardware to prevent electrochemical degradation. Kynar 500 coating on aluminum provides the same 30+ year coastal performance as the roof panels. 0.032″ gauge for base and counter-flashing provides sufficient rigidity for field forming and wind resistance at the parapet face; 0.024″ for coping flashing is appropriate as it is fully supported by the mortar bed and not subject to wind load. Pre-formed inside corners are mandatory — field-formed inside corners are the most common source of parapet flashing leaks and are not acceptable at this installation. ASTM C920 polyurethane at laps provides adhesive bond in addition to water seal, superior to sealant-free lapped joints. Alternatives rejected: copper flashing (cost premium, patina aesthetic no longer the design driver, galvanic risk at interfaces with aluminum gutter); 316 SS flashing (galvanic couple with aluminum system even with isolation; coating breach during field fabrication creates localized corrosion risk); aluminum without Kynar coating (bare aluminum oxidizes and pits in coastal salt air within 5–10 years).
